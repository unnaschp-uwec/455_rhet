Silence as Oratory

Silence brings the listener into the conversation. It causese them to ponder the meaning of what has been said. It adds gravity to what will be said (Rappaport 289).

The Enthymeme:
"Body of Proof," the premise that need
not be stated explicitly.

Example (below):
Pres. Obama's 13-second pause during his eulogy for Sen. Clementa Pinckney
